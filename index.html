FAQ

Innovate with Data Science

What is your expected response time for inquiries? ▾
I typically reply within 2–4 days. During intensive project phases, it may take up to a week — but I always follow up. LinkedIn is the fastest channel to reach me.
Which domains are you most excited to work in? ▾
I'm most drawn to LLM applications, agentic AI systems, graph-based intelligence, and gaming analytics. High-impact domains like fintech fraud detection, healthcare AI, and sports analytics are where I want to create real-world change.
Are you open to freelance or consulting work? ▾
Yes, selectively. I take on freelance AI/ML projects involving genuine research challenges or production deployment complexity. Reach out with your problem statement and timeline.
What problems can be solved with Data Science? ▾
Data science can address fraud detection in fintech, demand forecasting in retail, predictive maintenance in manufacturing, patient outcome modelling in healthcare, and personalisation in gaming. The key is pairing the right algorithm with the right problem — not applying ML indiscriminately.
What's your approach to LLMs and agentic systems? ▾
I treat LLMs as powerful reasoning engines best paired with structured tools, retrieval systems, and well-designed logic graphs. LangGraph gives me the control flow I need for reliable multi-step agentic behaviour — I'm wary of over-engineering simple tasks with agents when a direct call suffices.

Unleashing a few of my stories…

Models, mountains, and the boys

Ask me about graph neural networks and I'll talk for an hour. Ask about that 9.45 CGPA and I'll just shrug — late-night debugging sessions and early-morning football practice turned out to be a weirdly good combo. I started at TCS figuring out why purchase orders kept breaking, moved to explaining stock price RMSE at HSBC, and now I'm at Iolite teaching machines to catch fraudsters before they know they've been caught.

Off-screen, I'm probably on a football pitch, a few thousand feet up on a trek I underestimated, or trying — and often failing — to get the framing right on my camera. Most of my favourite memories don't have a model behind them, just good company, terrible trek-day decisions, and friends who somehow still agree to come along. Somewhere between an Olympiad gold in 2014 and shipping a fraud model with 98% F1-score, I realised I just like building things that get smarter over time — code included.
